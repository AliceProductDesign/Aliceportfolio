The Challenge

WAVE is a medical device designed to relieve menstrual pain, with a clinical study currently being planned to secure FDA clearance. WAVE is a device with real clinical credibility, but its brand undermined it.

Packaging, marketing, and digital presence were inconsistent, eroding trust at the exact moment the product needed to earn it.

The deeper challenge: repositioning a medical device to feel as human and empowering as the best consumer wellness brands, without sacrificing clinical authority.

The Solution

A full brand system redesign built around "warm authority" — the strategic middle ground between clinical coldness and wellness softness. New logo, visual identity, color and typography system, packaging, messaging framework, and social media direction.

Delivered as a complete, handoff-ready brand system that became the foundation for the website and all marketing going forward.

WAVE is an active client engagement. An ePRO (electronic patient-reported outcomes) app is currently in development as the next phase of the product, alongside a company website and e-commerce.

The Problem

The brand didn't match the product.

When I came onto the project, WAVE had no cohesive visual identity. Packaging, marketing materials, and digital presence were all pulling in different directions — some leaning clinical and cold, others generic and unbranded.

For a product targeting women managing real, ongoing pain, that inconsistency had a cost: it eroded credibility at the exact moment the brand needed to earn trust.

What the initial audit found

  • No cohesive branding or system
  • Disconnected packaging and collateral
  • Logo mark and wordmark didn't feel as if they belonged together
What I Designed

Logo & Visual Identity

Redesigned the logo to move away from generic medical iconography toward something cleaner and more ownable. The updated mark balances precision with softness, reflecting both the device's clinical efficacy and its role in everyday wellness.

Brand System

Color & Typography System

Built a palette that moved away from the stark whites and blues of medical design toward calmer, more considered tones — still clean and trustworthy, but emotionally warmer. Typography hierarchy was simplified to prioritize readability and approachability over clinical density.

Navy — Core
Pink — Warmth
Blue — Trust

The rebranding of WAVE focused on evolving the identity from just a clinical medical device into a warm, human-centered wellness brand. By redefining its visual system, core messaging, and user experience, the new identity brings emotional depth, trust, and clarity to a category often overlooked in women's health care.

Benefits & Value

WAVE provides drug-free, non-invasive relief for menstrual pain and other forms of pelvic discomfort. Its design prioritizes comfort, movement, and usability. With science-backed efficacy, WAVE offers a natural, reusable alternative to medication.

Brand Goals

WAVE aims to redefine how menstrual pain is managed and become a drug-free leading solution for pelvic relief. The brand is committed to making relief more accessible, inclusive, and sustainable.

Target Audience

Women, medical professionals, and women's health advocates. WAVE's primary users are women who experience cyclical or chronic pelvic pain, with a focus on menstruating individuals seeking alternatives to pharmaceuticals — including health practitioners who recommend WAVE as a trusted solution for pain relief.

Brand Mission — "Good Days, Every Day"

WAVE's mission is to empower people to have control of their wellness, every day of the month. We believe everyone deserves easy, effective relief without compromise. Through science-backed technology, intuitive design, and stigma-free storytelling, WAVE is reshaping what care can look and feel like — quietly powerful, deeply personal, and always human.

Packaging

Designed to live on a bathroom shelf, not in a hospital catalog.

Designed packaging that could sit on a bathroom shelf without looking like it came from a hospital supply catalog. Every decision — material feel, information hierarchy, and label language — was made to reinforce one idea:

This product respects you and the pain you're managing.

Messaging & Social

Stigma-free communication.

Developed a messaging framework built around stigma-free communication. Menstrual pain is chronically dismissed and under-discussed. The brand voice was written to name it directly, without euphemism, and without the performative positivity that makes wellness brands feel hollow.

Brand System for Handoff

A system complete enough to work without me in the room.

Delivered a complete brand system that a separate designer could use to build the website: meaning every decision had to be documented, scalable, and clear enough to work without me in the room.

The Outcome

From fragmented to unified.

The before/after tells the clearest story. WAVE went from a fragmented, inconsistent product presence to a unified brand with a distinct visual voice.

The new identity was strong enough to carry the product into the ACOG exhibit — one of the most important healthcare professional conferences in the US — and hold its own in that environment.

Reflections

What I'd Do Differently

With more time and access, I would have grounded the visual decisions in direct user research — specifically talking to women about how they discover and evaluate products for pain management, and what visual and tonal signals make them trust a medical device.

What This Project Proved

Designing for health is designing for trust. Every color choice, every word, every packaging decision either builds it or quietly erodes it. WAVE reinforced that the hardest design problems in health tech aren't always the technical ones, but also the emotional ones.
